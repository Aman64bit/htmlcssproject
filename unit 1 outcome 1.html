1.2 Describing methods, skills and resources needed to complete digital projects successfully – what do you need to make projects successful? What tools? What skills?:

Different projects require different tools, methods and skills. For web development, one could follow a simple method of conceptualising, planning, development and testing. When conceptualising you have to initially think of the possibilities, risks and challenges which may incur with the project. Also at this point certain key parameters are declared such as costs and time requirement. This is then reviewed and approved before moving on to the next stage.

Planning:

At this point a detailed plan is made to materialise the concept/idea. Design and development teams work with user experience and marketing to create requirements such as : design, initial user experience.

Development and testing:

The development process begins to turn the concept into reality, starting naturally with the back end. This is to create the architecture on which the front end will rely. A beta version is made to review and adjust.

Deployment:

This is a crucial step, upto this point the product has been tested and everything must be ready, this includes all the backend working properly and front end fully functional and upto quality standards, then finally it is deployed.

Tools and skills:

There are many options available to a developer to use. Some of the most popular are js, css, c#, html. These programming languages are a key tool and require the right skills, front end development would make use of css, html. While back end use would require the right tools such as python.

1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects – what causes digital projects to fail? Why?

There are many risks associated when working on a digital project, some of them include:

Lack of diversity in expertise:

having a team of digital programmers for a project but missing a business oriented team member to have some business expertise to act as bridge to manage customer needs to the back-end operations.

Strained digital strategy:

A company needs to have a well defined digital strategy and set what their overall goal is. This can help the project stay on focus rather than prioritizing one department's initiative above others. A real and very personal example is Microsoft getting hung up on the kinect when releasing the Xbox one, focusing efforts on motion detection, night vision and other features rather than focusing on game performance. being a larger box than the Ps4 (its main competition) and still requiring a large external power brick led to some damage towards the companies reputation as well as sales.

Overambition:

Many times companies get hung up on creating a perfect product. This often leads to the project being dragged on and on past deadlines, it is helpful to use the idea of “minimum viable product” or realising a beta version and improving on that.

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?

When working on digital projects one must plan and prepare for all the legal rules and regulations which may be in place. These laws and regulations are set in place to protect consumers rights such as full declaration of what information we store about them and what we do with that information. This may include emails, cookies, trackers, preferences, social media posts. On the 25th of may 2018, the GDPR (general data protection regulation) was implemented as law in the european union. This impacts how businesses and big tech companies collect and process information of eu citizens. The GDPR regulations dictate that consumers have: “The right to be informed, right of access, right to rectification, right to erasure/to be forgotten, right to restrict processing, right to data portability, right to object and rights in relation to automated decision making and profiling.” University of leicester
