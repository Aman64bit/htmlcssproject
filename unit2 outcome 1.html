1.5 Describing copyright and other constraints affecting websites

Copyright. When someone produces an original piece of work, may that be a song, poem or a product, then copyright laws dictates that work legally belongs to the creator. Anyone or any businesses looking to use that work must have consent from the creator. This helps people benefit from their own work rather than it being copied and used for any reason without consent from the creator. Websites must use content allowed by the rightful owner. 1.6 Describing access issues that need taking into account - describe issues that people with disabilities may face when using webpages and how we can overcome them.

There are many different types of disabilities, the definition of disability can vary from country to country and even different communities of people within those countries. A user can have one or more types of disabilities such as having little to no vision, impared hearing, amputees. Web developers have to cater to these disabilities. They can do this in a number of ways. Alt tags: alt tags can contain useful information which can be read by screen readers for people with impared vision. The alt tag should contain a description of the content, image, text ect. So if the user hovers over the an image of a fast car, The reader should read out a description like a speeding car. Subtitles and transcripts: When having a video on your page, there should be descriptive subtitles or a transcript for users with a lack of hearing.
